# Yoked — Nano Influencer Outreach List (Under 20K)

**Goal:** Gift a free year of Yoked Pro to small creators who'd be a natural fit. No strings attached — just get the app in their hands, let them try it, collect feedback, and if they love it they can share it organically.

**Why nano/small creators:** You're at 15 Instagram followers. Reaching out to someone with 500K followers will feel off and probably get ignored. But reaching out to a creator with 2K–15K followers? You're peers. They'll actually read your DM, actually try the app, and if they like it, their recommendation carries more weight with their audience because their audience trusts them like a friend, not a billboard. Nano influencers have 7x the engagement rate of mega-influencers.

**Your pitch in a nutshell:** Yoked is an AI personal trainer in your pocket for $4.99/mo. It builds personalized workout programs based on your goals, equipment, and schedule. Perfect for people who want structure but can't afford (or don't want) a human personal trainer.

## Tier 4: Hashtag Hunting Strategy (Find Your Own)

The truth is, the best nano influencers for Yoked are people actively posting right now who aren't on any list yet. Here's how to find them yourself:

### TikTok Hashtags to Browse
Search these hashtags and look for creators with 1K–15K followers who are posting consistently:

- **#fitmom** — moms documenting workouts
- **#postpartumfitness** — moms getting back into it after baby
- **#gymbeginner** — people just starting out
- **#fitnessnewbie** — literal beginners sharing their journey
- **#homeworkout** — people working out without a gym
- **#fitnessjourney** — transformation/progress content
- **#gymtok** / **#fittok** — general fitness community
- **#workoutapp** — people already reviewing/discussing fitness apps
- **#AIworkout** — people interested in AI fitness tools
- **#personaltraineralternative** — your exact target audience

### Instagram Hashtags
- **#fitmomlife** — daily fitness + motherhood
- **#momswholift** — moms who strength train
- **#beginnerfitness** — self-explanatory
- **#homegymworkout** — equipment-flexible workouts
- **#fitnessappreview** — people who review fitness tech

### What to Look For
When browsing these hashtags, look for creators who:
1. Have **1K–15K followers** (your sweet spot)
2. Post **consistently** (at least 2–3x/week)
3. Get **genuine comments** (not just emoji spam)
4. Talk about **needing structure** or **not knowing what to do at the gym**
5. Have tried other fitness apps and talk about what they liked/didn't like
6. Are in a **fitness journey** (before/during transformation, not already "done")

## Outreach Template

Here's a casual, no-pressure DM that works great at the nano level because it's founder-to-creator, not brand-to-influencer:

> Hey [Name]! I'm Mike — I built a fitness app called Yoked. It uses AI to create personalized workout programs based on your goals, equipment, and schedule (basically a personal trainer in your pocket for $5/mo).
>
> I've been watching your content and really like [specific thing — be genuine here]. I think you'd actually get a lot out of the app, and I'd love to give you a free year to try it out.
>
> Zero strings attached — no obligation to post or anything. I'm mostly just looking for honest feedback from people who actually know fitness. But if you end up liking it and want to share it, that'd obviously be awesome too.
>
> Want me to set you up?

**Tips for the DM:**
- **Personalize the [specific thing]** — mention a recent post of theirs by name. This is the difference between getting a reply and getting ignored.
- **Send from your personal account**, not a brand account. Founder DMs get way higher response rates than brand DMs at this level.
- **Don't follow up more than once.** If they don't reply, move on. There are thousands of nano creators out there.
- **Batch your outreach** — reach out to 10–15 people per week. Expect a 20–30% response rate at this level.
